The two towers mirror the original paper: encoder on the left, decoder on the right, connected in the middle where the encoder hands its output to the decoder's cross-attention. Everything is built from the sentence above, run through a small (but real) transformer — 8-dimensional vectors instead of hundreds, so every number stays visible.

Click a block — embedding, an attention layer, add & norm, anything — and this panel shows exactly what it computes for your sentence, in plain language and in the actual numbers.
